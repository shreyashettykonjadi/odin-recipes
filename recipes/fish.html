Mangalorean Fish Fry

Description

Mangalorean Fish Fry is a spicy and tangy seafood dish from the coastal region of Mangalore, India. Fresh fish is marinated in a flavorful blend of spices and then shallow-fried to perfection, resulting in a crispy exterior and succulent interior. This dish pairs excellently with steamed rice, neer dosa, or appam.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams firm fish fillets (e.g., pomfret, kingfish, mackerel)
  • 2 tablespoons red chili powder (preferably Kashmiri for vibrant color)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon cumin powder
  • ¼ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper powder
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind extract or white vinegar
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons rice flour (optional, for added crispiness)
  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil (for frying)
  • A few curry leaves (optional, for added flavor)
  • Lemon wedges and sliced onions for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare the Marinade:
    • In a bowl, combine red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, black pepper powder, ginger-garlic paste, tamarind extract (or vinegar), and salt. Mix well to form a thick paste. If the mixture is too dry, add a few drops of water to achieve a spreadable consistency.
  2. Marinate the Fish:
    • Clean and pat dry the fish fillets. Make shallow slits on both sides to allow the marinade to penetrate.
    • Evenly coat the fish with the prepared spice paste, ensuring it gets into the slits. If using, sprinkle rice flour over the marinated fish for extra crispiness.
    • Let the fish marinate for at least 30 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  3. Fry the Fish:
    • Heat coconut oil in a skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add curry leaves to the oil for added aroma (optional).
    • Place the marinated fish fillets in the skillet. Fry each side for about 3-4 minutes or until the exterior is crispy and golden brown, and the fish is cooked through.
    • Remove the fish from the skillet and place on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  4. Serve:
    • Transfer the fried fish to a serving platter. Garnish with lemon wedges and sliced onions.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice, neer dosa, or appam for a traditional Mangalorean meal.
